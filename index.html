At A Glance

My name is Razar (ɹˈɑːzə) , and I'm a newly certified amateur radio operator. Something about amateur radio's simplicity paired with its complexity sparked my curiosity. At first, I wondered "what is this amateur radio one speaks of." With the growth of the internet, this was not a difficult question to answer. 

Unlike many current operators at the moment, I have not hit senior status yet! Like how many operators began, I'm 15. My young age is a driving force towards constantly learning, because there's a lot I don't know that I want to know. This very driving force allowed me to self study for a couple months to obtain my Basic qualification in Canada.

In 2024, I officially became certified as a true Canadian amateur radio operator with Basic with Honours. Currently, most of my time on frequencies are spent listening to conversations made in my local city: Regina, SK.

Bit Of Background

Fireworks went off as millions in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia celebrated New Year's 2009. On the same day, a young Arakanese boy was born. That boy was me. Only 349 days later, they shipped me and my father to Canada on December 15, 2009. We lived in Surrey, British Columbia for two years until we move to Regina, Saskatchewan. I've lived in the prairie's capital ever since.

Ever since grade 3, I've been a programmer. It began with a simple book where I discovered HTML. This grew into learning CSS, which led to JavaScript, which eventually led to Python, C++, and multiple Node.js frameworks.

We are the stars. Every organ, mineral, vitamin, and internal structure is a culmination of interstellar matter coming together. I was only 13 when this epiphany hit me. What's The Plan?

Amateur radio is a hobby millions of people around the world participate in. Unlike other hobbies, many amateur radio operators participate not only because of what they already know, but the hope they learn something they don't.

I am no different. Ever since I was a toddler, I've been breaking apart toys and electronics hoping to understand concepts from the root. Walking into this new realm, there's a lot I want to learn. There isn't a small birdie telling me to pursue this hobby. The reason I'm here is because I'm truly passionate.

  1. Work DX to a different province, country, and continent
  2. Operate on HF frequencies
  3. Use moon bounce propagation
  4. Operate using a Yagi
  5. Communicate to the ISS
  6. Learn and obtain qualification for CW (morse)
  7. Climb an antenna
  8. Build a Quarter-Wave Ground Plane antenna
